A friendly introduction to the latest advances in the symbolic features
of Maude have appeared in Proc. LOPSTER 2020: [Symbolic Computation in
Maude: Some Tapas](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-68446-4_1).

In-depth presentations of Maude, and on how to use its most common
features, are available in the following books:

* M. Clavel et al.: [All About Maude - A High-Performance Logical
    Framework, How to Specify, Program and Verify Systems in Rewriting
    Logic](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-71999-1). LNCS 4350,
    Springer (2007)
* P.C. Ölveczky: [Designing Reliable Distributed Systems - A Formal
    Methods Approach Based on Executable Modeling in
    Maude](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4471-6687-0). Springer (2017)

Several bibliographies and surveys on rewriting logic and Maude have
been published:

* M. Clavel et al.: [Two Decades of Maude](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-23165-5_11). Logic,
    Rewriting, and Concurrency: 232-254 (2015). A pre-print is available
    [here](:papers/Two-Decades-of-Maude.pdf).
* J. Meseguer: [Twenty years of rewriting
    logic](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jlap.2012.06.003). J. Log.
    Algebraic Methods Program. 81(7-8): 721-781 (2012).
* N. Martí-Oliet, M. Palomino, and A. Verdejo: [Rewriting logic bibliography by topic: 1990--2011](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jlap.2012.06.001). J. Log. Algebraic Methods Program. 81: 782-815 (2012)
* N. Martí-Oliet and J. Meseguer: [Rewriting logic: roadmap and
    bibliography](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0304-3975%2801%2900357-7).
    Theor. Comput. Sci. 285(2): 121-154 (2002).
